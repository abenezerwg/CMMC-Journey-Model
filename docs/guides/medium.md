# CMMC Journey Path: Medium Enterprise (51–250 employees)

⚠️ **Disclaimer:** This is an unofficial, open-source guide. Use for educational purposes only.

## Overview
- Full scope (all CUI systems, multiple departments)
- C3PAO assessment required for CMMC certification
- **12–18 month timeline** (typical; realistic planning essential)
- ~$50K–$100K budget
- **Level 1 + Level 2 fully, Level 3 controls prioritized**

## Recommended Stages

### 1. Discover
- Business case and contract obligations analysis
- Executive buy-in and resource commitment
- ROI and risk assessment
- Identify all DoD contracts with CMMC requirements

### 2. Scope
- Complete asset inventory across departments
- CUI/FCI classification and data flow mapping
- Boundary definition and enclave decision if multiple systems
- Network diagram creation

### 3. Assess
- Detailed gap analysis across all **Level 1 and Level 2 controls**
- Verification against **320 Assessment Objectives (AOs)**
- POA&M planning with prioritization
- Control prioritization (Level 1 → Level 2 → Level 3)

### 4. Implement
- **Level 1 critical controls first**
- Parallel implementation of Level 2 controls
- Evidence collection and documentation
- Policy documentation and staff training across departments

### 5. Document
- Complete SSP covering all systems and departments
- Finalized POA&M with realistic timelines and milestones
- Evidence package organized by control family (AC, AU, CM, IA, IR, MA, MP, PE, PS, RA, SA, SC, SI)
- Supporting procedures and configuration documents

### 6. Certify
- Engage **Certified CMMC Assessor (CCA)** or **Certified CMMC Professional (CCP)** for pre-assessment review
- C3PAO selection and formal assessment engagement
- Address findings and remediate before formal C3PAO assessment
- Continuous compliance monitoring post-certification

## Tips
- Use structured project management tools and governance
- Ensure executive sponsorship and clear accountability
- Plan for resource allocation across multiple departments
- Establish clear POA&M acceptance criteria—include simple fixes with justified timelines
- Regular steering committee reviews to track progress
